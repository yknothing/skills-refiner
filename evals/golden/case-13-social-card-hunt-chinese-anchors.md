# Golden anchors — Case 13: Chinese targeted hunt for social-card skills

## What a strong answer must do

### Need interpretation
- Restate the need as: a skill that can turn source material such as articles, links, or media into a coherent set of multi-card, vertical, mobile-native share assets.
- Explicitly distinguish this from:
  - generic image generation;
  - generic PPT/presentation generation;
  - single-image poster skills;
  - generic summarization or copywriting skills.
- Recognize that "design感和美观" is not cosmetic here — it is part of the core requirement.

### Source strategy
- Demonstrate awareness that this category is likely to surface first on major skill platforms/apps, official app-owned skill repos, and X.
- Use those channels for discovery and GitHub or author repos for structural verification.
- Avoid acting as if GitHub search alone is enough for this use case.

### Quality assessment
- Evaluate whether a candidate can do all or most of the following:
  - ingest long-form or mixed-format source material;
  - extract and compress the right content;
  - sequence ideas across multiple cards rather than one output block;
  - respect vertical/mobile-native layouts;
  - show real design taste instead of template-level decoration.
- Distinguish between:
  - skills that make visually polished assets but do weak content extraction;
  - skills that summarize well but produce bland, non-design-aware outputs;
  - skills that are promising but still too generic for social-card workflows.

---

## Key judgment anchors

### A strong answer will
- Recommend a small shortlist with specific reasons for each pick.
- Clearly identify one starting pick.
- Flag at least one popular but off-target option, such as a generic PPT, image, or video generation skill that does not really solve the multi-card social-sharing problem.
- Note that this category may be strongest in adjacent ecosystems such as design/video/presentation-oriented skill hubs rather than in traditional coding-skill collections.

### A strong answer may also
- Note that the best current options may come from adjacent design ecosystems rather than "social card" skills labeled exactly that way.
- Identify a gap: many skills can generate pretty visuals or summaries, but fewer can jointly handle extraction, sequencing, and share-ready card design.
- Describe what an ideal skill would do if nothing fully meets the bar.

---

## Failure signals

Treat the answer as **failing** if it:
- Recommends generic image-generation or PPT skills without explaining why they fit the multi-card share workflow.
- Collapses the need into "make nice-looking images."
- Ignores design quality and focuses only on text summarization.
- Ignores X and platform/app discovery channels for a trend-sensitive space like this.
- Produces a broad list of media-generation tools without curation.
- Fails to mention any gap between current ecosystem offerings and the user's full requirement.

## Score floor to pass

- Need interpretation: ≥ 4
- Source strategy: ≥ 4
- Quality judgment: ≥ 4
- Curation quality: ≥ 4
- Overall: ≥ 3.8 average across all applicable dimensions
