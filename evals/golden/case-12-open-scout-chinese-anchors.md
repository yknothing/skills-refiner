# Golden anchors — Case 12: Open Scout in Chinese

## What a strong answer must do

### Mode control
- Operate in Open Scout mode, not Targeted Hunt.
- Do not produce a popularity ranking — the user explicitly requested otherwise.
- Organize discoveries by quality category: hidden gems, rising quality, mature and underrated.
- 把 X 和主流 skill 平台放在主要发现渠道的位置（treat X and major
  skill platforms as primary discovery channels）。

### Language discipline
- Output entirely in Chinese.
- Write in natural Chinese prose, not translated English.
- Keep commonly-used English technical terms in English (skill, prompt, workflow, agent).
- Use full-width Chinese punctuation.
- No empty transitions, no filler adjectives, no template openings.

### Anti-noise discipline
- Identify at least one overrated skill or pattern and explain why it is overrated.
- Do not include skills primarily because they are popular.
- Surface at least one skill or pattern that most users have not noticed yet.

### Evidence discipline
- Acknowledge that an ecosystem-wide scan necessarily works with less depth than a targeted evaluation.
- Adjust confidence appropriately — do not rate discovery-stage skills as "excellent" without structural evidence.
- Clearly separate direct observation from inference.

---

## Key judgment anchors

### A strong answer will
- Name specific skills or skill patterns with concrete quality evidence.
- Explain what makes each discovery genuinely noteworthy (not just "it's interesting").
- Identify at least one ecosystem gap or emerging space worth watching.
- Keep the output focused and dense — a scout brief, not a survey.

### A strong answer may also
- Compare the current state of the ecosystem to a previous state or to the user's likely expectations.
- Identify design patterns that are becoming more common across quality skills.
- Note specific authors or repositories that consistently produce quality work.

---

## Failure signals

Treat the answer as **failing** if it:
- Produces a popularity-ranked list (directly contradicts the user's request).
- Outputs in English or mixed Chinese-English prose.
- Uses AI-flavor Chinese: 套话开头, 空洞过渡词, 堆砌形容词.
- Recommends skills without any quality evidence.
- Fails to identify any overrated skill or pattern.
- Writes like a translated English article.

## Score floor to pass

- Anti-noise discipline: ≥ 4
- Language discipline: ≥ 4
- Quality judgment: ≥ 3
- Evidence discipline: ≥ 4
- Overall: ≥ 3.5 average across all applicable dimensions
